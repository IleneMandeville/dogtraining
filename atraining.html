General Information

The first thought that comes to mind when
Agility Dog Training is mentioned is a
dog show. This training style is infact
very beneficial to the dog and trainor
alike. This training style gives both he
owner and their dog some daily exersises
and new commands to use. The sport of dog
agility is only a few decades old. It was
first created to fill the gap of time
between dog shows. This dog training style
can closen the bond bettween an owner and
their dog and can be a lot of fun.

Some Tips to Agility Courses

  • Jumps are adjusted to the size of the dog
  • Tire jumps are adjusted to the size of the
    dog
  • A pause table is a piece of elevated land
    for the dog to stay at for a period of time
  • Weave poles are closely aligned poles for
    dogs to weave through
  • An A-frame is where a dog must ascend and
    descend while tking the right size steps
  • A dog walk is narrower and longer than an
    A-frame
  • A seesaw is where dogs must cross similar
    to the dog walk
  • Tunnels are a enclosed area with two open
    ends that can be straight curved or collapsed
